HireFlow gives recruiters a full end-to-end hiring workflow: create your company profile, post approved job listings, and let the AI rank incoming candidates by semantic match score — then move them through a structured pipeline with automatic candidate notifications at every stage.

<Note>
  All recruiter endpoints are protected by the `require_recruiter` role check. Your account must have the `recruiter` (or `admin`) role to access them. Jobs you post do not become visible to candidates until an admin approves them and their status changes to `LIVE`.
</Note>

## How the recruiter workflow works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register with the recruiter role">
    Sign up at `POST /api/v1/auth/register` and set `"role": "recruiter"` in your request body. You will receive a JWT access token to use in all subsequent requests.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up your company profile">
    An admin creates the company record. Once it exists, your user account is linked to that company via a `Recruiter` record. You can then view your company profile and update it at any time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a job posting">
    Submit a new job with `POST /api/v1/jobs`. The job starts in `DRAFT` status and moves to `PENDING_APPROVAL` automatically. It is not visible to candidates yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for approval">
    An admin or authorized recruiter reviews the job. On approval, the status becomes `LIVE` and the posting is immediately visible to job seekers. If rejected, a reason is recorded on the job.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review AI-ranked candidates">
    Once applications come in, fetch a ranked list of candidates with `GET /api/v1/jobs/{job_id}/candidates`. HireFlow uses semantic matching to order applicants by how well their resume fits the role.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move candidates through the pipeline">
    Advance or reject candidates stage by stage using `POST /api/v1/pipeline/move`. Every move is logged in the audit trail and the candidate is notified automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the job when filled">
    When the position is filled or cancelled, call `POST /api/v1/jobs/{job_id}/close` to set the status to `CLOSED`.
  </Step>
</Steps>
