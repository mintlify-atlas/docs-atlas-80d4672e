ATS stands for Applicant Tracking System — the automated software most companies use to filter resumes before a recruiter ever sees them. HireFlow scores your resume against ATS criteria and gives you a transparent report so you know exactly what to fix.

## What the ATS score measures

Your ATS score is a float from **0–100**. It combines five weighted components, each measuring a different aspect of how well your resume will perform in automated screening.

| Component | Max points | What it checks |
|---|---|---|
| Keywords | 35 | Presence of relevant industry and role-specific terms |
| Sections | 25 | Required resume sections (experience, education, skills, etc.) |
| Format | 20 | Absence of tables, columns, graphics, and non-parseable layouts |
| Length | 10 | Word count in the optimal 400–900 word range |
| Clarity | 10 | Sentence structure, action verbs, and overall readability |

The five components sum to a maximum of 100. HireFlow also assigns a letter grade:

| Score | Grade |
|---|---|
| 90–100 | A |
| 75–89 | B |
| 60–74 | C |
| 45–59 | D |
| 0–44 | F |

## Fetching your ATS score

Retrieve your current ATS score and full report from your active resume:

```bash
curl http://localhost:8000/api/v1/resume/me \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer <token>"
```

Or fetch the report for a specific resume by ID:

```bash
curl http://localhost:8000/api/v1/resume/{resume_id}/ats \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer <token>"
```

A typical response looks like this:

```json
{
  "score": 68.0,
  "report": {
    "grade": "C",
    "breakdown": {
      "sections": 20,
      "keywords": 22,
      "format": 16,
      "length": 7,
      "clarity": 8
    },
    "issues": [
      "Multi-column layout detected — may not parse correctly"
    ],
    "warnings": [
      "Missing certifications section",
      "Low keyword density for target role"
    ],
    "missing_sections": ["certifications"],
    "keyword_match": {
      "matched": ["Python", "REST API", "PostgreSQL"],
      "missing": ["Docker", "Kubernetes", "CI/CD"],
      "score": 22,
      "coverage": 0.43
    },
    "format_issues": [
      { "message": "Multi-column layout detected", "severity": "high" }
    ],
    "improvement_tips": [
      "Include these keywords from the JD: Docker, Kubernetes, CI/CD",
      "Remove tables and multi-column layouts — use single-column format",
      "Add missing sections: certifications"
    ]
  }
}
```

## Reading the report

### `breakdown`

Shows the raw score for each component. Use this to identify where you're losing the most points. A `keywords` score below 25 is the most common cause of a low overall score.

### `issues`

High-severity problems that directly harm ATS parsing — such as tables, multi-column layouts, or graphics. These are the most important to fix.

### `warnings`

Medium- and low-severity observations, such as missing optional sections or slightly low keyword density. Address these after resolving `issues`.

### `missing_sections`

Sections that were not detected in your resume. Common required sections are: work experience, education, skills, and contact information. Optional but beneficial sections include certifications, projects, and summary.

### `keyword_match`

When a job description is available (Premium), `matched` lists keywords found in both your resume and the JD, while `missing` lists JD keywords absent from your resume. `coverage` is the fraction of JD keywords matched (0.0–1.0).

### `improvement_tips`

A prioritized list of up to five actions you can take to raise your score. These are generated from your actual breakdown data — not generic advice.

## ATS screening and your pipeline

When you apply to a job, HireFlow runs ATS screening as the first automated gate:

- If your resume **passes** the ATS check, your application moves to `ATS_SCREENING` and then progresses to interview rounds.
- If your resume **fails**, your application moves to `ATS_REJECTED`. You can re-apply after uploading an improved resume.

<Info>
  The `ats_passed` field on your application record reflects whether you cleared this gate. You can see this in your application status.
</Info>

## Tips to improve your ATS score

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use a single-column layout">
    Multi-column resumes, tables, and text boxes confuse ATS parsers. Use a simple, linear layout with clear section headers. This is the single highest-impact change for a low format score.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Include industry-specific keywords">
    Review the job descriptions you're targeting and add the technical terms, tools, and skills that appear frequently. Don't stuff keywords — use them naturally within your experience descriptions. Premium users can run a JD-specific ATS score to see exactly which keywords are missing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Add all standard resume sections">
    Make sure your resume has clearly labeled sections for: Work Experience, Education, and Skills at minimum. Missing sections directly reduce your sections score.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Aim for 400–900 words">
    Too short suggests thin experience; too long suggests poor editing. Expand bullet points with quantified achievements (e.g., "reduced API latency by 40%") to hit the optimal range.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use strong action verbs">
    Start each bullet point with a verb: built, designed, optimized, led, reduced, increased. This improves your clarity score and makes the resume easier for both ATS and humans to parse.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  After making changes, upload your revised resume to run a fresh ATS analysis. Uploading a new file automatically deactivates the previous one, so your score will always reflect your latest version.
</Tip>
